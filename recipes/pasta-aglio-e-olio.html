Pasta Aglio e Olio

A classic Italian dish made with just 5 pantry staples — garlic, olive oil, pasta, red pepper flakes, and parsley. Simple, quick, and incredibly delicious.

⏱️15 minutes
👥Serves 2
🍝Main Course
🇮🇹Italian

Why You'll Love This Recipe

Pasta Aglio e Olio (garlic and oil pasta) is one of the most beloved quick pasta dishes in Italian cuisine. Originating from Naples, this dish has been a staple of Italian home cooking for centuries. What makes it special is its simplicity — no fancy ingredients, no complicated techniques, just a few pantry staples transformed into something truly magical.

This recipe is perfect for those late-night cravings, quick weeknight dinners, or whenever you find yourself with an almost-empty fridge. The key to a great aglio e olio lies in gently infusing the olive oil with garlic and red pepper flakes, then tossing it with perfectly cooked pasta and a splash of starchy pasta water to create a silky, emulsified sauce.

Ingredients

  • ✓ 200g (7 oz) spaghetti or linguine
  • ✓ 6-8 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • ✓ 60ml (1/4 cup) extra virgin olive oil
  • ✓ 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • ✓ Salt, to taste
  • ✓ Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • ✓ Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

💡 Ingredient tips: Use good quality extra virgin olive oil — it's the star of this dish. Fresh garlic is essential; garlic powder won't give the same results. Reserve some pasta water before draining!

Instructions

1

Cook the pasta. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti or linguine and cook according to package instructions until al dente (firm to the bite). Before draining, reserve about 1 cup of the starchy pasta water — this is crucial for the sauce!

2

Prepare the garlic. While the pasta cooks, heat the olive oil in a large skillet or pan over medium-low heat. Add the thinly sliced garlic and red pepper flakes. Cook gently, stirring frequently, until the garlic turns golden and fragrant — about 2-3 minutes. Do not let it brown too much or it will become bitter.

3

Combine pasta and oil. Drain the pasta and add it directly to the skillet with the garlic oil. Toss to coat the pasta evenly.

4

Create the sauce. Add a splash of the reserved pasta water (start with 2-3 tablespoons) and toss vigorously. The starchy water will emulsify with the oil to create a glossy sauce that clings to the pasta. Add more pasta water if needed until you reach your desired consistency.

5

Season and serve. Taste and season with salt if needed. Remove from heat, stir in the chopped parsley, and serve immediately. Top with grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

🔥 Pro tip: The secret to restaurant-quality aglio e olio is the pasta water. It contains starch that helps the oil and water emulsify into a creamy sauce without any cream or butter.

Variations & Additions

While the classic aglio e olio is perfect on its own, feel free to customize it with these additions:

  • ✓ Anchovies: Add 4-6 anchovy fillets to the oil with the garlic for a deeper umami flavor.
  • ✓ Prawns: Pan-sear some prawns separately and toss them in at the end.
  • ✓ Cherry tomatoes: Halve and quickly blister them in the pan before adding the garlic.
  • ✓ Lemon: Finish with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and lemon zest for brightness.
  • ✓ Breadcrumbs: Toast some breadcrumbs in the oil before adding the garlic for a crunchy topping.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use garlic powder instead of fresh garlic?

While you can, fresh garlic is highly recommended. Garlic powder won't infuse the oil the same way and lacks the aromatic depth that makes this dish special.

Why is my pasta aglio e olio not saucy?

The sauce comes from emulsifying the pasta water with the olive oil. Make sure to use enough starchy pasta water and toss vigorously. The sauce should be glossy and coat every strand.

Can I make this dish ahead of time?

Aglio e olio is best served immediately. However, you can prep the garlic and measure out ingredients in advance. Cook the pasta and make the sauce just before serving for the best results.

Is this recipe vegan?

Yes! The classic aglio e olio is naturally vegan. Just skip the optional Parmesan cheese topping, or use a vegan alternative.
