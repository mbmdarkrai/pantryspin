## Ingredients

- 1 lb (400g) spaghetti or linguine
- 6-8 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 1/2 cup extra virgin [olive oil](https://www.amazon.co.uk/s?k=extra+virgin+olive+oil)
- 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)
- Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

## Equipment

- [Large pasta pot](https://www.amazon.co.uk/s?k=large+pasta+pot)
- [Wooden spoon for stirring](https://www.amazon.co.uk/s?k=wooden+spoon+set)
- [Colander](https://www.amazon.co.uk/s?k=kitchen+colander)
- [Non-stick frying pan](https://www.amazon.co.uk/s?k=non-stick+frying+pan)

## Instructions

### Step 1: Prepare the Water
Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Add a generous pinch of salt.

### Step 2: Cook the Pasta
Add the pasta to boiling salted water and cook according to package directions until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water before draining.

### Step 3: Infuse the Oil
While the pasta cooks, gently heat the olive oil in a large frying pan over medium-low heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the garlic is fragrant but not brown (burnt garlic will taste bitter).

### Step 4: Combine Pasta and Oil
Add the drained pasta to the pan with the garlic oil. Toss well to coat. If the pasta seems dry, add some of the reserved pasta water, a little at a time, until you reach the desired consistency.

### Step 5: Season and Serve
Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and black pepper. Divide among serving bowls and top with parsley and Parmesan if desired. Serve immediately.

## Tips

- **Don't Burn the Garlic**: Keep the heat low and watch carefully. Burnt garlic tastes bitter.
- **Use Quality Olive Oil**: This dish is all about the quality ingredients. A good extra virgin olive oil makes a huge difference.
- **Pasta Water is Key**: The starch in pasta water helps create a light sauce that clings to the noodles.
- **Preparation**: Have all ingredients sliced and ready before cooking, as everything comes together quickly.

## Variations

- Add fresh chilli pepper instead of dried flakes
- Toss in some anchovy paste for a more complex flavour
- Include toasted breadcrumbs for a crispy texture
- Add fresh seafood like prawns or clams
